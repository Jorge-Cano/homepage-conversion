Universal Frustration Patterns

These friction points appear in every converter session — none of them are on the homepage. They live on the product listing page and in the checkout flow.

🔴 Critical
Pickup Location Not Clickable

Users rage-clicked the pickup address 9–18× trying to open it in maps or copy it. It renders as static, non-interactive text. Occurred in every sampled session without exception.

Rage intensity
9–18× per session
Make the address a tappable link that opens in Google Maps
🔴 Critical
"No Cars Found" — No Fallback

Two of three sessions hit a city with zero inventory (Nashville, Tucson). No nearby city suggestions, no alternate dates shown — just a modal that says "Uh Oh" and closes. This is the #1 non-converter drop-off point.

Drop-off risk
2 of 3 sessions
Surface nearby available cities + "try different dates" prompts inline
🟠 High
Total Price Is Not Interactive

Users rage-clicked the price display ($40, $360, $900) expecting a cost breakdown — per day rate, fees, taxes. 9–13 clicks per occurrence. The element is display-only with no tooltip or expand behavior.

Rage intensity
9–13× per session
Add a toggleable price breakdown: per day × days + fees + taxes
🟠 High
Login Interrupts Flow at Unpredictable Points

Login surfaced mid-homepage, mid-search (on favoriting), and again at checkout entry. One session required two separate authentication events — adding 60–90 seconds of friction to an already long path.

Up to 2× per session
Persistent auth state + guest favoriting; one auth gate at checkout entry
🟡 Medium
Date Picker Requires 6 Separate Dropdowns

Month / Day / Time for pickup + Month / Day / Time for dropoff = 6 sequential fields. 35% of converters used "Select Different Dates" on the product page, meaning the dates entered on the homepage didn't hold or didn't work.

🟡 Medium
Delivery Address Requires 5 Click-Input Cycles

Every session shows 5 separate click → input-change sequences on the delivery address field in checkout payment. This may be a click-to-focus issue where fields don't activate on the first click.

So What This Means
  • The homepage is not the problem. These frustrations are entirely downstream — on the product page and in checkout. Optimize those before touching the homepage.
  • The two Critical issues (Pickup Location, No Cars fallback) affect every single converter. Fixing them removes the most common rage-click patterns from 1,516+ sessions immediately.
  • The High issues (price breakdown, login flow) extend session time unnecessarily. Each fix directly reduces time-to-checkout and lowers abandonment risk at the summary step.
